Abstract

In practical scenarios, a human-interactive robot needs to precisely understand its environment and swiftly adjust to any changes within it. Current works distill rich semantics from 2D foundation models into implicit representations of 3D scenes; yet they necessitate relearning the entire scene when the scene changes, leading to significant time expenses and an inability to manage the updated scenes.

In this paper, we propose Semantic-aware 3D Gaussians Splatting (SeGS) to explicitly model the scenes with rich semantic information, achieving fast incremental semantic reconstruction for newly updated scenes. Specifically, SeGS first models the Structure-from-Motion (SfM) points as 3D Gaussians and then introduces semantic features for each 3D Gaussian to capture the semantic information around the corresponding point. The SeGS model is jointly optimized through scene reconstruction and feature distillation from the CLIP embeddings. Due to such explicit modeling, SeGS can quickly respond to scene changes by adjusting the semantic-aware Gaussians of the changed points in an incremental manner and empowers robots to perform language-driven tasks in updated environments.

Extensive experiments on continuous tasks show that SeGS is capable of quickly reconstructing the updated scenarios, enabling immediate execution for the next task. It thus enables cost-effective, efficient, and swift determination of irregularly moved object positions, thereby making it possible for robots to seamlessly cooperate with humans.
